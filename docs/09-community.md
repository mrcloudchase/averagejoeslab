## Getting Help 🙋‍♂️

### Best Practices for Getting Support

| Step | Best Practice | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Search Discord history** | Your question might be answered already |
| 2 | **Be specific** | "When I run X, I get error Y" vs "X doesn't work" |
| 3 | **Share code/screenshots** | Helps others help you faster |
| 4 | **Show what you've tried** | Demonstrates effort and helps narrow down issues |
| 5 | **Be patient** | Community members are volunteers |
| 6 | **Say thank you** | Appreciation encourages more help |

### Question Templates

#### For Technical Issues
```
**Problem:** [Brief description]
**What I'm trying to do:** [Your goal]
**What I expected:** [Expected result]
**What actually happened:** [Actual result]
**Error message:** [Exact error text]
**What I've tried:** [Your attempts]
**Environment:** [Colab/local, Python version, etc.]
```

#### For Learning Questions
```
**Topic:** [What you're learning about]
**Specific confusion:** [What exactly you don't understand]
**Context:** [What phase/project you're working on]
**What you've read/watched:** [Resources you've tried]
**Specific question:** [Exact question you need answered]
```

## Study Groups 📚

### Finding Study Partners

| Preference | How to Find |
|---|---|
| **Same phase** | Check phase-specific channels |
| **Same timezone** | Post in `#study-groups` with your timezone |
| **Same interests** | Look for people working on similar projects |
| **Same pace** | Find others with similar time availability |

### Study Group Activities
- **Paper reading sessions** - Read and discuss papers together
- **Code review** - Help each other debug and improve code
- **Project collaboration** - Work on projects together
- **Accountability check-ins** - Keep each other motivated
- **Mock presentations** - Practice explaining your work

### Starting a Study Group
1. **Post in `#study-groups`** with your interests and availability
2. **Set regular meeting times** that work for everyone
3. **Choose communication method** (Discord voice, video calls, etc.)
4. **Define goals** - What do you want to accomplish together?
5. **Start small** - 2-3 people is often ideal

## Mentorship Program 👥

| Phase | What You Can Ask For |

## Community Guidelines 📋

### Our Values
- **Inclusivity** - Everyone is welcome regardless of background
- **Respect** - Treat all community members with kindness
- **Growth mindset** - Mistakes are learning opportunities
- **Collaboration** - We succeed together
- **Open science** - Share knowledge freely

### Expected Behavior
- ✅ **Ask questions** - No question is too basic
- ✅ **Help others** - Share your knowledge and experience
- ✅ **Be patient** - Everyone learns at their own pace
- ✅ **Give credit** - Acknowledge others' contributions
- ✅ **Stay on topic** - Keep discussions relevant to research

### Unacceptable Behavior
- ❌ **Harassment or discrimination** of any kind
- ❌ **Spam or self-promotion** without community value
- ❌ **Sharing copyrighted content** without permission
- ❌ **Gatekeeping** - Making others feel unwelcome
- ❌ **Academic dishonesty** - Plagiarism or cheating

## Community Health 💚

### Maintaining a Positive Environment
- **Welcome newcomers** - Help them feel included
- **Celebrate diversity** - Different perspectives strengthen us
- **Address conflicts** - Speak up if you see problems
- **Support struggling members** - Offer encouragement
- **Model good behavior** - Be the community member you want to see

### Reporting Issues
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:
1. **Document the incident** - Screenshots, timestamps, etc.
2. **Report to moderators** - Use `@Moderator` or DM
3. **Provide context** - What happened and when
4. **Follow up** - Check that the issue was resolved

### Self-Care
- **Take breaks** - It's okay to step away when needed
- **Set boundaries** - Don't feel obligated to help everyone
- **Ask for support** - Reach out when you're struggling
- **Celebrate progress** - Acknowledge your own growth
