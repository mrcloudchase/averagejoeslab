Getting Help 🙋‍♂️​

Best Practices for Getting Support​

StepBest PracticeWhy It Works
1Search Discord historyYour question might be answered already
2Be specific"When I run X, I get error Y" vs "X doesn't work"
3Share code/screenshotsHelps others help you faster
4Show what you've triedDemonstrates effort and helps narrow down issues
5Be patientCommunity members are volunteers
6Say thank youAppreciation encourages more help

Question Templates​

For Technical Issues​

**Problem:** [Brief description]
**What I'm trying to do:** [Your goal]
**What I expected:** [Expected result]
**What actually happened:** [Actual result]
**Error message:** [Exact error text]
**What I've tried:** [Your attempts]
**Environment:** [Colab/local, Python version, etc.]

For Learning Questions​

**Topic:** [What you're learning about]
**Specific confusion:** [What exactly you don't understand]
**Context:** [What phase/project you're working on]
**What you've read/watched:** [Resources you've tried]
**Specific question:** [Exact question you need answered]

What to Expect​

  • Casual atmosphere - No formal presentations required
  • Open discussion - Bring questions, share progress
  • Peer learning - Learn from others' experiences
  • Expert guests - Occasional visits from research professionals
  • Recording available - Can't attend? Catch up later

Study Groups 📚​

Finding Study Partners​

PreferenceHow to Find
Same phaseCheck phase-specific channels
Same timezonePost in #study-groups with your timezone
Same interestsLook for people working on similar projects
Same paceFind others with similar time availability

Study Group Activities​

  • Paper reading sessions - Read and discuss papers together
  • Code review - Help each other debug and improve code
  • Project collaboration - Work on projects together
  • Accountability check-ins - Keep each other motivated
  • Mock presentations - Practice explaining your work

Starting a Study Group​

  1. Post in #study-groups with your interests and availability
  2. Set regular meeting times that work for everyone
  3. Choose communication method (Discord voice, video calls, etc.)
  4. Define goals - What do you want to accomplish together?
  5. Start small - 2-3 people is often ideal

Mentorship Program 👥​

For Mentees (Getting Mentorship)​

PhaseWhat You Can Ask For
Phase 1Help reading papers, study guidance
Phase 2Code review, debugging help, tool recommendations
Phase 3Research guidance, experimental design feedback
Phase 4Career advice, publication guidance, networking

Finding a Mentor​

  • Post in your phase channel describing what help you need
  • Be specific about your goals and time commitment
  • Be respectful of mentors' time and expertise
  • Come prepared to mentorship sessions with specific questions

For Mentors (Providing Mentorship)​

Your ExperienceHow You Can Help
Completed Phase 1Help newcomers with paper reading
Completed Phase 2Code review and technical guidance
Completed Phase 3Research methodology and project planning
Completed Phase 4Career guidance and community leadership

Becoming a Mentor​

  • Volunteer in #phase4-community when you're ready
  • Start small - Help answer questions in earlier phase channels
  • Share your experience - Write about your journey
  • Be patient - Remember what it was like when you started

Community Guidelines 📋​

Our Values​

  • Inclusivity - Everyone is welcome regardless of background
  • Respect - Treat all community members with kindness
  • Growth mindset - Mistakes are learning opportunities
  • Collaboration - We succeed together
  • Open science - Share knowledge freely

Expected Behavior​

  • ✅ Ask questions - No question is too basic
  • ✅ Help others - Share your knowledge and experience
  • ✅ Be patient - Everyone learns at their own pace
  • ✅ Give credit - Acknowledge others' contributions
  • ✅ Stay on topic - Keep discussions relevant to research

Unacceptable Behavior​

  • ❌ Harassment or discrimination of any kind
  • ❌ Spam or self-promotion without community value
  • ❌ Sharing copyrighted content without permission
  • ❌ Gatekeeping - Making others feel unwelcome
  • ❌ Academic dishonesty - Plagiarism or cheating

Community Events 🎉​

Regular Events​

  • Monthly showcases - Present your projects to the community
  • Paper reading clubs - Discuss interesting research together
  • Coding challenges - Fun programming exercises
  • Guest lectures - Learn from industry and academic experts
  • Hackathons - Collaborative coding events

Special Events​

  • Research conferences - Attend virtual conferences together
  • Workshop series - Deep dives into specific topics
  • Community challenges - Group projects and competitions
  • Celebration events - Recognize achievements and milestones

Organizing Events​

Interested in organizing an event?

  1. Propose in #community-events with your idea
  2. Get community feedback and gauge interest
  3. Plan logistics with community moderators
  4. Promote the event across relevant channels
  5. Follow up with recordings and resources

Recognition & Achievements 🏆​

Community Badges​

Earn recognition for your contributions:

  • 🌟 Helpful Helper - Consistently helps others
  • 📚 Paper Pioneer - Shares interesting research
  • 🔧 Code Contributor - Helps with technical issues
  • 👥 Mentor Master - Guides newcomers successfully
  • 🎯 Project Pro - Completes impressive projects

Spotlight Features​

  • Member of the month - Recognition for outstanding contributions
  • Project highlights - Showcase exceptional work
  • Success stories - Share your journey and inspire others
  • Expert interviews - Share your expertise with the community

Beyond Discord 🌐​

Other Community Platforms​

  • GitHub Organization - Collaborate on code projects
  • Research Blog - Share longer-form content
  • Twitter/X - Follow @AverageJoesLab for updates
  • LinkedIn Group - Professional networking
  • YouTube Channel - Video tutorials and presentations

External Communities​

We encourage participation in broader research communities:

  • Papers With Code - Contribute implementations
  • Hugging Face - Share models and datasets
  • Kaggle - Participate in competitions
  • Reddit - r/MachineLearning, r/AskScience
  • Academic Twitter - Follow researchers in your field

Community Health 💚​

Maintaining a Positive Environment​

  • Welcome newcomers - Help them feel included
  • Celebrate diversity - Different perspectives strengthen us
  • Address conflicts - Speak up if you see problems
  • Support struggling members - Offer encouragement
  • Model good behavior - Be the community member you want to see

Reporting Issues​

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

  1. Document the incident - Screenshots, timestamps, etc.
  2. Report to moderators - Use @Moderator or DM
  3. Provide context - What happened and when
  4. Follow up - Check that the issue was resolved

Self-Care​

  • Take breaks - It's okay to step away when needed
  • Set boundaries - Don't feel obligated to help everyone
  • Ask for support - Reach out when you're struggling
  • Celebrate progress - Acknowledge your own growth

Join us - research is better together! 🤝
