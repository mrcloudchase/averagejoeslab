Current Projects & Initiatives

Average Joes Lab is actively working on several research initiatives that demonstrate the power of democratized science. Our projects span multiple domains and welcome contributions from researchers at all levels.

Active Research Projects​

🤖 Open AI Safety Research​

Status: Active | Contributors: 8 researchers | Started: January 2025

We're developing community-driven approaches to AI safety that don't require massive computational resources. Our focus is on creating interpretability tools and safety frameworks that independent researchers can use and contribute to.

How to contribute:

  • Join our weekly AI Safety working group
  • Contribute to our open-source interpretability toolkit
  • Participate in our community peer review process

🔬 Democratized Peer Review System​

Status: Beta Testing | Contributors: 12 researchers | Started: December 2024

Our flagship project is building an alternative to traditional academic peer review. This system values merit over credentials and provides transparent, community-driven quality assurance for research.

Current features:

  • Anonymous, merit-based review process
  • Community reputation system
  • Open review comments and discussions
  • Integration with preprint servers

🌐 Citizen Science Methodology Framework​

Status: Development | Contributors: 6 researchers | Started: November 2024

We're creating standardized methodologies that enable citizen scientists to conduct rigorous research without institutional support. This includes protocols, tools, and quality assurance measures.

Research areas:

  • Environmental monitoring protocols
  • Social science survey methodologies
  • Data collection and analysis standards
  • Community validation processes

Upcoming Initiatives​

📊 Open Research Data Platform​

Launch: Q2 2025

A decentralized platform for sharing research data with proper attribution and version control. Designed to make high-quality datasets accessible to independent researchers.

🎓 Research Mentorship Network​

Launch: Q1 2025

Connecting experienced researchers with newcomers to provide guidance and support. This network will facilitate knowledge transfer and help build the next generation of citizen scientists.

🔧 Research Tools Collective​

Launch: Q3 2025

Community-developed, open-source tools specifically designed for independent researchers. From statistical analysis to experiment design, we're building the toolkit that democratizes research.

How to Get Involved​

For New Researchers​

  1. Start with our Research Engineering Learning Path
  2. Join our Discord community
  3. Attend our weekly project meetings
  4. Contribute to documentation and testing

For Experienced Researchers​

  1. Lead a new research initiative
  2. Mentor newcomers in your area of expertise
  3. Contribute to our peer review system
  4. Help develop research methodologies

For Everyone​

  • Spread the word about democratized research
  • Provide feedback on our tools and processes
  • Share your research through our platform
  • Collaborate on interdisciplinary projects
